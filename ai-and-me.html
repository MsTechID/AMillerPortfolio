AI & Me: Safe, Smart, and Ethical

This four-lesson mini-unit engages students in meaningful scenario-based learning to help them use generative AI in ways that promote digital citizenship and educational ethics.

Project Overview

Audience: 5th–6th grade students

Responsibilities: Instructional designer (lesson planning, time mapping, activity design, visual design)

Format: In-person instruction with printable materials

Duration: Four 40-minute sessions

The Problem

Students are increasingly exposed to generative AI, but most elementary classrooms lack structured, age-appropriate guidance on their use. This results in confusion around how AI works, what information is safe to share, and how to use AI responsibly in learning contexts.

The Solution

After consulting with district stakeholders, I designed an in-person, four-lesson mini-unit. The unit supports peer discussion and guided exploration of realistic scenarios. Students collaboratively create an “AI Code of Honor,” fostering shared ownership of ethical AI use.

Process

Analysis

Following the ADDIE model, I performed a needs analysis of learners, outcomes, and context. Interviews with teachers and curriculum directors informed key concerns around AI usage and learner needs.

Design

Based on analysis insights, I established three core learning goals:

  • Distinguish safe from unsafe AI interactions
  • Identify common “red flag” patterns in AI output
  • Recognize appropriate vs. inappropriate AI use in learning

The culminating task — a collaboratively created AI Code of Honor — serves both assessment and ownership purposes.

Development

Materials and teacher guides were developed with clarity and accessibility in mind. A usability pilot informed revisions prior to broader implementation.

Planned Implementation & Evaluation

While the unit is fully developed, a full classroom pilot is planned for a future instructional cycle. I will observe engagement, review contributions to the AI Code of Honor, and collect teacher feedback to refine pacing, clarity, and complexity.
