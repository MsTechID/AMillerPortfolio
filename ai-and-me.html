AI & Me: Safe, Smart, and Ethical

AI & Me is a four-lesson instructional mini-unit designed for 5th–6th grade students. The unit introduces generative AI through age-appropriate, scenario-based learning focused on digital citizenship, ethical use, and critical thinking.

Students explore what AI is, how it works at a conceptual level, what information is safe to share, and how to distinguish between appropriate learning support and academic dishonesty. The unit culminates in a collaboratively created AI Code of Honor, reinforcing shared responsibility and ethical decision-making.

  • Audience: 5th–6th grade students
  • Format: In-person instruction with printable materials
  • Duration: Four 40-minute sessions
  • Role: Instructional Designer

Process

The Problem

Students are increasingly exposed to generative AI, but most elementary classrooms lack structured, age-appropriate guidance on its use. As a result, students are often unclear about how AI systems work, what information is safe to share, and how to use AI for learning without crossing into cheating or plagiarism.

The Solution

Following consultation with district stakeholders, I designed an in-person, four-lesson mini-unit that supports peer discussion and collaborative learning. Students work through realistic AI safety and ethics scenarios and collaboratively create an AI Code of Honor, giving them shared ownership of ethical commitments.

Analysis

Using the ADDIE model, I began with an analysis of the instructional problem, learners, learning environment, and desired outcomes. I interviewed classroom teachers and the district curriculum director to identify key concerns regarding AI use for this age group. I also conducted student interviews to understand prior knowledge, perceptions, and preferences.

Design

Based on analysis findings, I established three primary learning goals:

  • Students can distinguish between safe and unsafe information to share with AI tools.
  • Students can identify common AI “red flag” patterns (urgency, rewards, suspicious links).
  • Students can recognize the difference between appropriate learning support and cheating.

To assess these goals, I designed a culminating performance task: a collaboratively created AI Code of Honor. This task serves as both an authentic assessment and a shared commitment to responsible AI use.

Development

After establishing core design decisions, I developed student-facing materials and teacher guides. All materials were reviewed for clarity and accessibility, and a small usability pilot was conducted to identify areas for refinement prior to full implementation.

Planned Implementation & Evaluation

At the time of this portfolio submission, the unit has been fully designed and developed but has not yet completed a full in-class pilot. A classroom implementation is planned for a future instructional cycle.

During implementation, I plan to observe student engagement, review student contributions to the AI Code of Honor, and collect informal teacher feedback. These data points will inform revisions to pacing, clarity, and scenario complexity.
